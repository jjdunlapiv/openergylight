Operation Dynamo

Operation Dynamo is a phased project to emplace Advanced Medium Mobile Power Sources, known as AMMPS, Improved Environmental Control Units, or IECUs, and Power Distribution Illumination Systems, or PDISEs, at selected sites in the Combined Joint Operations Area - Afghanistan.

The focus is on remote locations and other locations selected by U.S. Forces Afghanistan. PM MEP anticipates a reduced reliance on spot power generation and using between 40 to 60 percent less fuel once the equipment has been emplaced and units trained to operate and maintain it.

Operation Dynamo - Before

A single Force Provider Shower was powered by a 100 kW TQG. The peak power draw was 23 kW.

Operation Dynamo - After

The Force Provider Shower is now powered by a 30 kW AMMPS. The same 30 kW AMMPS will power a new Force Provider Latrine once operational.

Leadership Responsibilities

The Army value of Duty means being able to accomplish tasks as part of a team, fulfilling your obligations. You fulfill your obligations as a part of your unit every time you resist the temptation to take "shortcuts" that might undermine the integrity of the final product. It is your duty to ensure Operational Energy Security through responsible leadership.

  • Train your Soldiers on operational energy practices and policies.
  • Evaluate your soldiers' energy practices.
  • Communicate to your Soldiers your evaluation of their energy behaviors/practices.
  • Measure the results of energy informed behavior.
  • Develop a training and assessment plan for effective energy use.
  • Plans should include a separate section for training and for assessments.
  • Training section needs to include energy related actions Soldiers need to learn or improve.
  • Assessment section must explain how each action in the training section will be evaluated.
  • Plan should include feedback to Soldiers and report to chain of command as required.
